Green Tea Muffins

Moist and Tender Vegan Green Tea Muffins

This recipe makes the most delicious green tea muffins. Nobody will know they are vegan! They are ao tender and moist and flavorful. The hint of almond goes perfectly with the delicate matcha tea flavor. If desired, you can a frozen fruit such as raspberries or blueberries.

Making these is super simple! Start by preheating your oven to 350°F and lining a muffin tin with muffin papers. You will need two bowls. One is for the dry ingredients (white flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and matcha tea powder), and one is for the wet ingredients plus sugar (sugar, oil, almond and vanilla extracts, non-dairy milk, and non-dairy yogurt).

You simply put the dry ingredients in one bowl, whisk together the sugar with the wet ingredients in the other bowl, and then combine the two once your oven is preheated! Gently fold in the frozen berries last if desired, and scoop the batter into the muffin cups. PRO TIP: Putting batter into the tin is very tidy if you use an ice cream scoop. You can even decorate the muffins with slivered almonds. Viola, it's time to pop them in the oven. Bake for 20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

Ingredients

    Bowl One (dry ingredients)

  • 1¾ cups all purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon baking soda
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 4 teaspoons culinary grade green matcha tea powder

    • Bowl Two (wet ingredients)

  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ⅓ cup canola oil
  • ½ cup non-dairy yogurt
  • ⅔ cup non-dairy milk
  • ½ teaspoon almond extract
  • ¼ teaspoon vanilla extract

    • Reserved to Add Last:

  • ⅔ cup frozen berries such as raspberries or blueberries
  • ¼ cup slivered almonds to sprinkle on top

Steps

    Bowl One (dry ingredients)

  1. Sift the flour, measure, and place it into the dry ingredient bowl.
  2. Add the baking powder, baking soda, and salt to the flour.
  3. Add the matcha tea powder.
  4. Stir with a fork until the color is a uniform green.

    5. Bowl Two (wet ingredients)

  5. Place the sugar into the wet ingredient bowl 2.
  6. Add the canola oil and whisk the sugar and oil until well blended.
  7. Add the non-dairy milk and non-dairy yogurt, and both the extracts.
  8. Mix well.

    9. Wait Until Oven is Preheated to 350°F

  9. Line your muffin tin with muffin baking cup papers.
  10. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients.
  11. Stir with a rubber spatula until just blended.
  12. Fold in the frozen berries (if desired).
  13. Using an ice cream scoop, drop batter into lined muffin tin cups.
  14. Sprinkle the top of each muffin with slivered almonds (if desired).
  15. Place the muffin tin in the oven and bake for 20 minutes (or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean).
  16. Let freshly baked muffins cool for 5 minutes in the tin and then transfer to a wire rack to finish cooling.

Enjoy!

This recipe makes 12 muffins.
