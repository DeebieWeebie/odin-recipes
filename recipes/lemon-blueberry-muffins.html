Lemon Blueberry Muffins

Moist and Tender Vegan Lemon Blueberry Muffins

This recipe makes the most delicious lemon blueberry muffins. Nobody will know they are vegan! They have a crispy top yet are tender and moist on the inside. Lemon pairs exceptionally well with blueberries to bring out the full flavor of the fruit.

Making these is super simple! Start by preheating your oven to 400°F and lining a muffin tin with muffin papers. You will need two bowls. One is for the dry ingredients (white flour, baking soda, and salt), and one is for the wet ingredients plus sugar (sugar, oil, lemon extract, white vinegar, and non-dairy milk or non-dairy yogurt).

You simply put the dry ingredients in one bowl, whisk together the sugar with the wet ingredients in the other bowl, and then combine the two once your oven is preheated! Gently fold in the blueberries last and scoop the batter into the muffin cups. PRO TIP: Putting batter into the tin is very tidy if you use an ice cream scoop. Viola, it's time to pop them in the oven. Bake for 20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

Ingredients

    Bowl One (dry ingredients)

  • 2 cups all purpose flour
  • 1½ teaspoons baking soda
  • ½ teaspoons salt

    • Bowl Two (wet ingredients)

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ⅓ cup canola oil
  • 1 cup non-dairy milk or non-dairy yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon lemon extract
  • 1 tablespoon white distilled vinegar

    • Reserved to Add Last:

  • 1½ cups frozen or fresh blueberries

Steps

    Bowl One (dry ingredients)

  1. Sift the flour, measure, and place it into the dry ingredient bowl.
  2. Add the baking soda and salt to the flour.
  3. Stir with a fork until well blended.

    4. Bowl Two (wet ingredients)

  4. Place the sugar into the wet ingredient bowl 2.
  5. Add the canola oil and whisk the sugar and oil until well blended.
  6. Add the non-dairy milk or non-dairy yogurt, lemon extract, and white vinegar.
  7. Mix well.

    8. Wait Until Oven is Preheated to 400°F

  8. Line your muffin tin with muffin baking cup papers.
  9. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients.
  10. Stir with a rubber spatula until just blended.
  11. Fold in frozen or fresh blueberries.
  12. Using an ice cream scoop, drop batter into lined muffin tin cups.
  13. Place the muffin tin in the oven and bake for 20 minutes (or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean).
  14. Let freshly baked muffins cool for 5 minutes in the tin and then transfer to a wire rack to finish cooling.

Enjoy!

This recipe makes 12 muffins.
